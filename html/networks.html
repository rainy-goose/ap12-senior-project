The Structure of the Internet

Section 3: Networks

There are two general types of networks: public and private.


Public Networks

Public networks are generally able to sustain more traffic than private networks. The primary use of a public network is to allow many networked devices to connect to the internet. Businesses use public networks to keep their company information and inner workings private, to ensure they are not leaving themselves open to hostile attacks.

Private Networks

Private networks are usually designed to be small, but can scale up quite dramatically based on requirements. The primary use of a private network is to ensure security and safety between all networked devices. One example of a private network is the SDW network, where there is a global device whitelist. This means that only allowed devices are permitted to access the network, and all other devices cannot connect.
