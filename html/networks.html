The Structure of the Internet

Section 3: Networks

There are two general types of networks: public and private.


Public Networks

Private Networks

Private networks are usually small, but can scale up quite dramatically based on requirements. The primary use of a private network is to ensure security and safety between all networked devices. One example of a private network is the SDW network, where there is a global device whitelist. This means that only allowed devices are permitted to access the network, and all other devices cannot connect.
